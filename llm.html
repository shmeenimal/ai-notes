LLM

Claude or ChatGPT don't understand a single word you write. They just guess, really well.

An LLM is trained on a vast amount of text data. When you ask it something, it doesn't look up an answer — it predicts the next word, then the next, based on patterns from training. If it was only trained on facts about raccoons, it would be an expert on raccoons and completely lost with everything else.

Training is simple in concept. Show the model a sentence, ask it to guess the next word, correct it if wrong. Billions of times. That's it. Do that enough times and it becomes very good at predicting text. Not because it understands, just because it recognized enough patterns.

Since its only job is to predict the next word, it will always produce an answer, even when it shouldn't. There is no "I don't have enough data for this." It just predicts the best it can, and sometimes that means confidently making things up. This is called hallucination.

Unlike a function that always returns the same output for the same input, LLMs are not deterministic. Ask the same question twice, get a different answer. That's by design, not a bug.

How you write your input matters. The clearer and more specific you are, the better the prediction. Think of it like writing a spec. Vague spec, vague code. Good spec, good code.

And always verify. The model has no idea if it's right or wrong. It just sounds confident either way.
